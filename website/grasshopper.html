DAVIDE LOMBARDI

Inverted pyramid pavillion - Grasshopper
Valencia (ES)

This is the result of a course about the use of Rhinoceros with the plugin Grasshopper. It is located in the garden of the Polytechnic of Valencia, for a competition for finding the covering of the “Inverted pyramid”. Its structure is resolved with 16 ribs arranged in a circle and divided into 19 planes intersecting with 19 rings and 18 semicircles; its maximum height is 3.9 m. ICD-ITKE Research Pavilion, by Jan Knippers in Stuttgard, for the idea of form and Custore Pavilion, by Anna Dobek and Mateusz Wojcicki in Poland, for the structure have been taken as reference. Form and structure were selected to arrive at the idea of something that, going down, takes the slope of the hill where it is located. From this starting point the roof has been modified as if it were pushed in the center so that the effect of a structure born from the lowest point of the inverted Pyramid is obtained and, going up, it will support itself following the curves of the ground.
